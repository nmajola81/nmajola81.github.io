About me:

Hello! My name is Nomusa Mbali Majola and I currently reside in Cape Town. I hold a Bachelor of Science degree from the University of the Western Cape. I have over 10 years’ experience in the insurance sector, specializing in product development, pricing and underwriting. I also provide consultancy services to a boutique law firm to which I provide strategic and operational oversight in the management of cyber security related legal matters. I have a company that is in the process of developing an application that provides supportive ecosystems for black-owned businesses and entrepreneurs, providing them with a platform to market themselves and access infrastructure that will allow them to build and manage their businesses. It addresses the structural obstacles and systemic barriers that hinder black entrepreneurs’ efforts to start and sustain their businesses.

My objective in registering is to improve on my knowledge base, specifically looking at governance, and policy-making strategies to mitigate cyber security threats and vulnerabilities. I am looking forward to sharpening my technical cyber security skillset, specifically utilising OO methodologies to code systems that will identify and prevent breaches.

1. Launching into Cyber-Security

This was the first module in the course. The course aimed to introduce the field and relevant concepts.



2. Security and Risk Management

As the second module in the course, this module brought together a series of fascinating topics: risk assessment and management; supply chain modification and management; and cyber-security.

3. Network Security                 

This module adds vital tools to the cyber security toolkit in the form of vulnerability assessment strategies, vulnerability audits and some mitigation techniques.

4. Secure Software Development

This module delves into the heart of cyber security by examining the ways in which programming languages, code and software can be insecure and how to address these issues.

5. Research Methods and Professional Practice

A exciting module that aims to formally introduce students to the world of research, along with crucial aspects of research such as the ethics of computing and professional practice.

6. Secure Systems Architecture

A module that delved into the heart of the design of complex systems and how to secure such systems.
